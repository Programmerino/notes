North Korea fires projectiles into sea for second week running

North Korea has fired three unidentified projectiles off its eastern coast early Monday, a week after firing two short-range missiles. The projectiles flew up to 200 km and reached 50 km in altitude, South Korea’s military said. In the past 10 days, North Korea has said leader Kim Jong-un supervised two rounds of live-fire artillery exercises in its first weapons tests since late November.
