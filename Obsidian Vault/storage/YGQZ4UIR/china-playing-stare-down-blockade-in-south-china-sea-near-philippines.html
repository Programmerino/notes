China playing “Stare Down” blockade in South China Sea near Philippines

By Uriel | March 9, 2020 - 9:21 am |March 9, 2020 China, CIA, Communist Agenda, Department of State (State Dept), Dept. of Defense (DoD), Donald J. Trump, Evil Left, LEFTY MEDIA:, MILITARY:, NATIONAL SECURITY:, Russia, South China Sea, U.S. Foreign Policy, United Nations, US CONGRESS:

The Chinese Military are getting bolder about throwing up shipping lane blockades around the largest Spratley Island called Thitu. For over a year, every time Philippine fishermen or US military have been in those waters, Chinese military ships have circled the island threateningly as they block the few hundred people on the island and any who would enter its waters.

As this video from April 2018 by the BBC shows, airplanes are being challenged constantly in international airspace.

.

U.S. Navy Sails 7th Fleet Near Disputed Islands in The S.C.S for the First Time in 2020 – January 28, 2020

.

Now that Filipino President Rodrigo Duterte has decided to discontinue the US Visiting Forces agreement under the Military Defense Pact, the Chinese are leveraging their push in the region.

According to the Asia Maritime Transparency Initiative (AMTI) through Center for Strategic & International Studies (CSIS):

China has maintained an almost constant militia presence around Thitu Island, the largest of the Spratly Islands occupied by the Philippines, for over 450 days according to satellite imagery analyzed by AMTI. China first deployed militia vessels around Thitu in December 2018 in response to Philippine efforts to repair the island’s runway and undertake other upgrades. Since then, the militia fleet has fluctuated in size but rarely, if ever, disappeared. As a result, the Philippines has made very little progress on its planned upgrades. But there are signs that could be changing.

AMTI was able to count vessels on 208 out of 459 days. They did state that might not have been all that should have been counted because much of the area at the times were hidden under cloud cover. The count shows that at a minimum they believe an average of 18 Chinese ships are trolling around Thitu each day.

.

.

From all indications, Duterte has every intention of cozying up to China and Russia giving up far more than he realizes in the process. The fear is that the minute China believes it has a stranglehold on the South China Sea all of the islands and the shipping lanes will be shut down then either ransom or fees for “passage” even through possible international waters will be forced on shipping and on air space.

At that point new routes and ports will have to be found on the eastern side of the Philippines and the freedom of the Filipino citizens will be lost. Most citizens do not want Chinese dominance but Duterte is thinking with his ego and pocketbook rather than about citizens. By September 2019, it was reported that Duterte had already visited with the Chinese eight times.

He has been seemingly playing both ends against the middle in a cat and mouse game. The problem with that is the Chinese are deadly serious about their end vision for the region which has little to do with the wishes of the people on the Philippine Islands or any other countries. They have totally ignored international courts, U.N. demands, and especially U.S. diplomatic efforts.

–00-

This is why I am skeptical every time Trump talks about a “good relationship” or friendship with China. He may think he has some control but physical evidence suggests otherwise. We constantly see examples of how they are pushing the edge.

It is also why we should all be extremely worried about our national debt to China. So far because the U.S. has not appeared weak under Trump, the Chinese have held off slightly from pushing their weight around against us.

With the sell out of our country to the Chinese and their owning so many businesses and so much debt in the U.S thanks to the Clintons, Bush, and Obama, this is becoming more of a strain every day. Challenging in the South China Sea is only one area where they are making their presence not only known but felt as they “loan shark” their way around vulnerable countries and high value assets around the globe.

The set up now is simply waiting the right bully moment or demanded right to have our country in an all out war with China economically, financially, or militarily. At this point, that would leave us in a very vulnerable position on all sides with China having relations with Cuba, South and Central America, the Middle East, and now shutting down the South China Sea. That is not a comforting thought.

–Uriel–
