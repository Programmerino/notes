Preparing yourself for a new job interview? Nailing the interview should be all you have to focus on, but in the wake of the pandemic, the interviewing landscape has changed dramatically. Whereas just a few months ago, you would visit an office in-person to pitch your skills, now you’ll likely be jumping on a Zoom call instead.

Interviewing virtually comes with its own set of challenges, like making sure the space behind you isn’t a mess, trying to read a room that’s been reduced to just a chat window, and more. But, don’t worry, we’re here to help. Below are tips and tools you can use to navigate the shifting interviewing landscape and hopefully land a job in this new normal.

1. Look the part

Now that interviews are being done virtually via Zoom, Hangouts, etc., you’ll need to make sure your background looks presentable. No interviewer wants to see that towering pile of dirty clothes lurking in the back of your room. That’s where the Hello Backgrounds: Video & Images Package comes into play.

The bundle features 90 different videos and more than 300 images to set for your digital background on Zoom. Microsoft Office, or Skype. You can choose from a cozy study room to a sophisticated board room or any of the many other options available. Either way, virtual meetings just became a lot easier to look the part.

2. Sharpen your skills

Now that your background is set, prepping for an interview will help you land the job you want in a highly competitive job market. Whereas before, things like reading the room and leveraging body language to get your point across could give you an advantage, those elements aren’t particularly relevant when the interview is being done virtually.

That’s why it’s especially important to hone in on your interviewing skills and really sell yourself during the call. Outlining everything from types of interviews to “The Art of the Sale,” the Job Interview Skills Training Course can teach you valuable skills that can give you an edge in just one hour. Normally priced at $199, it’s currently marked down to just $14.99 USD.

3. Optimize your inbox

After you made your business pitch to a new company, sending a polished thank you note can go a long way toward being successful, and staying organized will make sure any follow-up email doesn’t get lost in the shuffle. You can streamline your inbox with a lifetime subscription to DarwinMail’s Professional Plan.

This secure inbox tool can give you greater control over your Gmail inbox. It can help you better navigate your inbox with different categories like sender, subject, or date by using the Bundle feature or sort through your emails in bulk. You can snooze some emails that you want to tackle later and even take back the contents of certain emails with the Undo Send feature.
